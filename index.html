We've got what you need

To the fellow foodies out there obsessed with keeping track:

There are plenty of apps to find and mark restaurants that you've been to and love (or hate). But none of them seem to satisfy all the basic needs of organizing places of interest. Foursquare has a great database of places, but you can only check in on Swarm. And neither has a good way of viewing the places you've been, or adding notes of your own. Yelp only allows you to check in or "bookmark" places, with no concept of organizing these bookmarked spots. You can star places, or create your own map on Google maps, but again, it is totally lacking in organization and structure. None of these allow you to create your own personal rating without publishing a public review. I've even resorted to Airtable to keep track of everything and be able to add my own notes, photos, and statuses. What ends up happening is that I use ALL of these in conjunction and it's insane and impractical.

Goldly provides a better way to keep track of all the places you've been, find new places, keep a personal rating system, store photos, and more.

You have a complete database at your fingertips. Restaurant tracking functionality of Foursquare, Swarm, Yelp, Google maps, and Airtable combined and neatly packaged in one app. Finally, something that satisfies the obsessive restaurant tracker in you.
